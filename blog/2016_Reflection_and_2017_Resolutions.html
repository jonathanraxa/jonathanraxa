2016 Reflection and 2017 Resolutions

Posted by Jonathan Raxa on January 2nd, 2017

I learned A LOT this year and with plently of mistakes made along the way in probably each and every aspect of my life. However, mistakes are no longer considered mistakes to me, they are "learning opportunites" as the general optomistic would say.

It's true though, the saying that the best learning comes from your mistakes revealed itself through this year. I was quite the experimentor this year, learning and growing in my mind and my body. I observed people, learned from books, watched videos, and in small ways I took action.

This year I know will be different. Everything I was able to learn in 2016 will now be put into practice on a deeper level. Activites ranging from meditation, graditude journaling, goal setting, writing, health and fitness, reading, public speaking, etc, are all things that I have already dipped my toes in and implemented in my life in some way shape or form. This time is different though, this time I have a better grasp of the importance of these things and how much they can effect my life in so many positive ways.

2016 Reflection - What I Learned and Questions to Begin My New Year.

Questions

  • Why did I stop writing?
  • Why did I stop meditating half way through the year?
  • Why was I afraid of people?
  • Why am I afraid of failure?
  • Why was I depressed?

Answers?

  • Why did I stop writing?
    • I began blogging towards the end of my schooling. Once school got busy, something had to give and the first thing that went was my writing. I'm finished with school, now it's time to get back into it and set myself a goal.
  • Why did I stop meditating half way through the year?
    • It was the same with writing. Meditation seemed to become something that just got in the way of my utilization of time and energy.
  • Why was I afraid of people?
    • If there was anything I felt fear of the most it would have to be people. Now this isn't a fear that keeps me in the corner of the room wishing I could just blow up into oblivion, it's a fear of what people thought about me. The thoughts and emotions of others influenced my actions. I would almost cater to someone based on what they thought about me or what I PRECIEVED from them. For the record, in a lot of wayps I do still do this, BUT I no longer succumb to their emotions, instead, I FOCUS on the other person rather than try to influence what they thought about me (this seems obvious but it really wasn't).
  • Why am I afraid of failure?

"ACTION isn't just the effect of motivation; it's also the CAUSE."
- Mark Manson

2017 Goals and Resolutions (Time to Reinvent)

This year is a new year, a time to start and begin anew. I never looked at a new year to be a time to start fresh, but being so inspired by so many mentors and after finally graduating, I feel as though I can take my life back.
